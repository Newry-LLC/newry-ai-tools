# Universal SoF Standards

These rules apply to all SoF work regardless of mode. The Pyramid Principle is the evaluative framework — these standards capture only what is Newry-specific or requires explicit calibration.

---

## Core Standard

Every headline and bullet must make a claim — an assertion about what is true, what the data shows, or what the client should conclude. Descriptions of Newry's process, how the work was organized, or general market context do not belong in the SoF.

---

## Headline Length

Newry slide title boxes fit approximately 150–175 characters. When a slide or screenshot is provided, check actual available space visually — a consultant may have written a short headline where more room exists. In either case, treat 150–175 as the working range: don't write shorter than necessary, and don't exceed what fits.

---

## Coverage by Stage and Audience

- **Final client readout**: All major conclusions must appear. A reader who sees only the SoF should not be surprised by a significant finding in the body.
- **Interim / progress update**: Findings so far and open questions. Full synthesis not required.
- **Weekly update**: Key developments since the last update only. Currency, not completeness.
- **Internal / working document**: Lower bar. Methodology content and status tracking are acceptable. Apply client-facing standards only if this is a precursor to a client deliverable.
