# Defensibility checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing any TAM analysis intended for boards, investors, or sophisticated buyers. Flag items that need strengthening and ask the user to provide additional data where possible.

A fully defensible analysis should pass all items in Section A and most items in Section B. Document any failures and your mitigation.

---

## Section A — Non-negotiable (must pass)

**Market definition**
- [ ] The market being sized is defined precisely — specific product/service, customer type, geography, and time period are all stated
- [ ] Inclusions and exclusions are explicit (what is NOT in this TAM)
- [ ] TAM, SAM, and SOM are calculated and distinguished
- [ ] The key unit metric is defined and used consistently throughout ($, volume, customer count)

**Sources**
- [ ] Every material data point has a named, verifiable source
- [ ] No material assumption rests solely on a Tier 4 source
- [ ] All sources are dated and within acceptable age (see source quality standards)
- [ ] Sources are cited with enough detail that a reviewer could verify them independently

**Triangulation**
- [ ] At least 2 independent methods were used (top-down + bottom-up minimum)
- [ ] Results from different methods are presented side-by-side, not hidden
- [ ] Divergence between methods is explained, not glossed over
- [ ] A reconciled range is presented (not a single point estimate)

**Math**
- [ ] Every formula is shown explicitly
- [ ] Every input value is stated with its source
- [ ] Arithmetic is correct (verify)
- [ ] The result is expressed as a range with a central estimate

---

## Section B — Best practice (flag gaps but not disqualifying)

**Assumptions**
- [ ] Confidence levels (High / Medium / Low) are assigned to key assumptions
- [ ] The top 3 assumptions driving the estimate are identified
- [ ] A sensitivity analysis shows what ±30% change in key assumptions does to the result

**Comparables**
- [ ] At least one comp transaction or public company revenue figure was found to cross-check scale
- [ ] The analysis has been compared to any publicly stated market size claims by competitors or investors

**Customer economics**
- [ ] Customer willingness to pay has been estimated (value-theory or interview-based)
- [ ] Bottom-up spend-per-customer is grounded in real pricing data (not purely hypothetical)

**Niche market rigor**
- [ ] Industry-specific sources were used (trade publications, associations) — not just general analyst reports
- [ ] Regulatory or certification requirements that gate the addressable market have been considered
- [ ] Geographic concentration of the customer base has been considered (e.g., salmon farming concentrated in Norway, Chile, Scotland)

**Presentation**
- [ ] The analysis distinguishes between "what the market is today" vs. "what it could be if the solution achieves full penetration"
- [ ] Limitations and caveats are stated honestly (not buried)
- [ ] The intended audience (board, investor, sales) is reflected in the level of conservatism and caveating

## Common investor objections — pre-empt these

These are the questions a sophisticated investor or board member is likely to ask. Ensure the analysis has answers before presenting.

1. "How did you define the market? Why not broader / narrower?"
   → Market definition section must answer this with a rationale

2. "Where does that [key number] come from?"
   → Every key figure needs a named source

3. "You used [Analyst Firm X]. Their methodology is opaque — what's your primary source?"
   → Always triangulate analyst figures against Tier 1/2 sources

4. "Your bottom-up and top-down are very different. Which do you believe and why?"
   → Reconciliation section must address divergence explicitly

5. "What if your [customer count / pricing / penetration] assumption is wrong?"
   → Sensitivity analysis must cover this

6. "What's your SAM? TAM is nice but I want to know what's actually reachable."
   → SAM must be calculated with explicit filters and rationale

7. "How does this compare to what [Competitor X] says about the market size?"
   → Comparable transactions / comps section should address this

8. "What's the fastest-growing segment? What's driving it?"
   → Growth dynamics should be noted, especially for projected TAM

---

## Scoring guide

Count passes in Section A:
- 12/12: Analysis is fully defensible — proceed to finalize
- 10–11/12: One or two gaps — identify and close before presenting to board/investors
- <10/12: Material gaps exist — do not present as investor-grade; label as "preliminary" and specify what is needed to strengthen it

Count passes in Section B:
- >10/13: Best-in-class analysis
- 7–10/13: Solid analysis with room for improvement — note which items to address
- <7/13: Analysis is directional only — recommend additional research before high-stakes use
