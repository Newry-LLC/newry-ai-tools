According to the Newry Ladder, here is what it says about the job description and responsibilities for a Project Manager:

---

SUMMARY DESCRIPTION (Consultant Development Continuum)

Project Manager
- Leads day-to-day project delivery and workplan execution
- Coaches teams and translates plans into results
- Builds client credibility while balancing quality, speed, and economics
- May own small firm initiatives
Typical timing: 2–4 years

---

ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES (Detailed)

- Manages projects to budget, scope, and proposal specifications
- Leads project launch and workplan development, including issue analysis and project tools
- Delegates work and coaches team members on analysis and core consulting skills
- Creates and reviews deliverables and underlying analyses
- Presents to clients and supports client discussions
- Teaches the consulting process to junior teammates

---

COMPETENCY AREAS

Client Management
- Builds trust-based relationships and provides thoughtful advice, not just information
- Communicates credibly with clients and contributes meaningfully to client discussions
- Supports client decision-making and demonstrates growing ability to influence outcomes

Critical Thinking & Problem Solving
- Contributes meaningfully to Thought Leadership with help from ED
- Plans analyses and leads structured thinking with limited guidance
- Uses workplans, issue trees, and frameworks to drive clarity and direction
- Delivers high-quality insights grounded in sound business judgment

Coaching and Development
- Coaches and develops team members through feedback and delegation; committed to team's and personal learning goals
- Transfers core consulting skills and Newry ways of working
- Addresses performance issues constructively

Execution / Project Leadership
- Manages time, priorities, and workstreams effectively to deliver on commitments
- Balances quality, scope, and project economics through careful planning and trade-offs
- Anticipates risks and adjusts plans proactively to keep projects on track

---

CHARACTERISTICS OF EXCELLENCE

- Raises the performance of the team through effective player-coach leadership
- Able to execute projects profitably and on schedule
- Generates accurate, defensible recommendations that influence client decisions
- Identifies opportunities to improve how Newry serves clients

Indicator of Excellence: Sought out by clients and Newry leaders; receives strong feedback in the form of follow-up work and positive debriefs
