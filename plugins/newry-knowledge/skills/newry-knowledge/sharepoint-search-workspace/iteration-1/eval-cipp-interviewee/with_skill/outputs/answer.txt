Three interviewees mentioned CIPP resin in the Alta project interviews:

1. Sarah Lapkowicz (Account Manager, Northeast/Pittsburgh-based)
Sarah gave the most detailed account. She identified filled CIPP resin as Alta's most important product gap and named it her single highest-priority addition to the lineup. Alta used to lead the CIPP market with a filled resin product but exited — the reason is unclear even internally (possible causes: pricing or manufacturing issues keeping granular filler in suspension). Competitors currently offer filled resin; CIPP fabricators strongly prefer it. Sarah identified two potential CIPP prospects near Pittsburgh she cannot convert because Alta only offers unfilled resin. She also noted that Julie (East RSM) had the same finding at the system level with Insitu Form. Quote: "That would be, like, your number one — this would help me add business in the process."

2. Tim Pepper (Senior Research Chemist / Research Director)
Tim mentioned CIPP in the context of Alta's low-styrene MMA resin R&D project. He flagged CIPP as a target application for the new formulation (alongside SMC/transportation), noting that CIPP is one of the markets where styrene odor complaints are most acute (banned in Oregon/Washington/California for sewer relining due to neighborhood odor during curing). He also identified CIPP-volume grades as part of the broader commodity VER opportunity where Alta's cost structure should give a decisive advantage.

3. Steve Councell (Account Manager, Wisconsin/Illinois/Kentucky/Michigan/Indiana territory)
Steve mentioned CIPP as part of a broader infrastructure growth theme, noting it had recently moved onto Alta's approved markets list after years of being avoided. Quote: "We used to be told to stay out of that. Didn't fit at the time. But now we are, and I'm really glad we're getting into that marketplace." He flagged it alongside data centers, chip plants, utility poles, rebar, and bridge decking as the clearest infrastructure growth opportunities from a field sales perspective.

Summary: Sarah Lapkowicz is the primary and most detailed source on CIPP resin as a near-term commercial loss. Tim Pepper and Steve Councell corroborate CIPP as a material opportunity from R&D and field sales perspectives respectively.
