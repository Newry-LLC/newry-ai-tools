Yes — Alta has secondary research that covers styrene, though not a dedicated styrene regulation document. The coverage appears in the initial secondary research deck under two topics:

1. Styrene as a cost driver for UPRs
The deck notes that "styrene cost pass-through limits pricing power" for unsaturated polyester resins (UPRs), which are Alta's largest revenue segment (60.4% of revenue). This is framed as a structural headwind that "caps margin upside" in the UPR segment and contributes to the historically negative CAGR of (2)–(3)% from 2018–2024.

2. Styrene-free regulation as a tailwind for gelcoats
Under the Gelcoat CAGR section, the deck identifies "low-VOC / styrene-free regulatory tailwind" as a key growth driver, specifically calling out the EU Green Deal as pushing "formulation shift industry-wide." This is listed alongside marine recoat cycles and wind blade gelcoat specs as a forward demand driver for the gelcoat segment (3–5% forward CAGR).

There is no standalone secondary research document focused specifically on styrene regulation — no dedicated regulatory analysis covering OSHA PELs, EPA NESHAP rules, REACH/SVHC status, or EU occupational exposure limits. The existing coverage is market-context only (cost pressure on UPRs, formulation-shift tailwind for gelcoats).

If a deeper regulatory analysis is needed, that would be a gap in the current secondary research corpus.
