# Vision — Newry AI Program

**Horizon:** May 2027
**Version:** v0.1

## Today

On a typical morning, an associate opens last week's interview transcripts — a dozen expert calls, hundreds of pages — and starts the familiar process. Ctrl+F for each recurring theme. Log every instance in a spreadsheet, row by row. Work that tabulation into a first-draft synthesis. Map the synthesis back to the issue tree. By mid-afternoon, a VP's review surfaces gaps that send a third of it back. The work is good, eventually, but late. Mechanical effort dominates the week; the judgment work — what we actually think, what the client should do — gets squeezed into the last days before the read-out. VPs live in review loops when they should be in client rooms.

## May 2027

A typical morning looks different. An associate opens a first-draft synthesis the system produced overnight from last week's fieldwork — structured to their issue tree, evidence mapped to sub-hypotheses, gaps explicitly flagged, quote-level traceability intact. They don't review it to rewrite it. They review it to *think* about it. Their first real task of the day is a judgment call: what do we actually believe, and what should the client do.

The associate role has changed in kind. Associates lead more interviews, shape more hypotheses, and spend more time inside client conversations, because the drafting, structuring, and formatting that used to absorb their days happens at system speed and Newry-grade quality. Seniors spend their time on architecture and narrative, not reconstruction. VPs are in more client rooms, not more review loops.

Firm knowledge behaves differently too. Methodology, prior project IP, reusable analytical patterns — all queryable, all compounding. Every engagement makes the next one faster and sharper.

## What shifts at the firm level

The unit economics of delivering a Newry project are materially different. The same team runs deeper engagements — or more engagements in parallel — at a higher quality floor than before. Capacity decouples from headcount; margin expands on the same work.

## What this commits us to

This is not a future where AI does the consulting. It is a future where AI does the work that doesn't deserve human judgment, so humans can do more of the work that does.

Two commitments follow: freed capacity gets spent on sharpened client thinking and deeper relationships — not absorbed into more projects at the same cadence. And the firm actively invests in leveling up people's ability to do the hardest parts of the work.

## Beyond May 2027

A further ambition: Newry builds and ships net-new AI capabilities — product lines that don't exist in the offering today. Client-facing AI systems in pricing, supplier optimization, market intelligence, or other domains where AI creates direct commercial leverage. This remains an option, not a commitment — to be addressed once the May 2027 foundation is in place.
